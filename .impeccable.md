## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: Citizens checking what their council discussed — typically after a meeting, on phone or desktop
- **Secondary**: Municipal staff (clerks, elected officials) managing transcriptions, minutes, and decisions — mostly desktop during work hours
- Both audiences need to find information fast with minimal friction

### Brand Personality
**Cool, trustworthy, open.**

OpenCouncil treats civic data with the craft and clarity of a modern product — not the dryness of a government portal or the urgency of an activist campaign. It earns trust through transparency (open-source, GPL v3) and through design that feels considered, not performative.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Clean, confident, warm. Modern efficiency with civic warmth — closer to Linear or Vercel than to a municipality website
- **About/sales page**: Rich motion, visual storytelling, personality. This is the expressive surface
- **Product (app)**: Calmer, more utilitarian. Information-dense screens should feel effortless, not flashy. Motion is purposeful (feedback, orientation), not decorative
- **Anti-references**: Activist sites (urgent, loud, cause-driven), bureaucratic portals (gray, dense, lifeless), flashy startup dashboards (gratuitous gradients, dark mode gamer aesthetics)
- **Theme**: Light mode primary. Dark mode supported but not the default
- **Corners**: Sharp (radius: 0rem) — intentional, gives a clean editorial feel
- **No colors to avoid**, but orange (#FC550A) is the signature — use it for action and emphasis, not everywhere

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element should make information easier to find. If a design choice doesn't help the user, remove it. Dense data (transcripts, subjects, speaker contributions) needs breathing room and clear hierarchy.

2. **Warmth without noise** — The interface should feel welcoming and human, not clinical. Use the orange accent, rounded avatars, and micro-interactions to add warmth — but never at the cost of readability or speed.

3. **Two speeds** — The about/sales page can be expressive (motion, storytelling, visual richness). The product UI should be calm and utilitarian. Know which mode you're in.

4. **Mobile-real** — Citizens check meetings on their phone after the fact. Mobile isn't a scaled-down desktop — it's the primary context. Design for thumb reach, scanning, and intermittent attention.

5. **Transparency as aesthetic** — OpenCouncil's mission is openness. The design should reflect that: visible structure, honest hierarchy, no hidden complexity. The interface should feel like it has nothing to hide.
